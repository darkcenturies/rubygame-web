0.1n Jul 18, 2026

Milestone: the shop redesign.

  • New Ruby Exchange section at the top of the shop menu: the currency pair alone — emerald 1.03 / 0.97, emerald block 9.27 / 8.73. One click to convert rubies in either direction.
  • The menu is reorganized and symmetric: Resources & Treasures, Building, and Farming & Food on one row; Combat & Tools, Decoration & Dyes, and Spawners on the next; Storage, Music, and Everything Else below. Resources & Treasures now holds every journey sellable (echo shards, nether star, beacon, heart of the sea, dragon breath) alongside the ores.
  • 311 dead listings that could be neither bought nor sold were removed, and the Magic and Misc sections are gone entirely — their few live items were relocated to fitting sections.
  • Every section lists its items cheapest to most expensive.
  • All listings are single-unit: prices are per one item and you choose the amount in the buy screen. Batch displays (per 4, per 9, per 32) are gone.
  • Closed purchase loopholes: filled buckets (a free iron bucket with extra steps), black and green dye, chains, plain firework rockets and trial keys can no longer be bought. The premium cosmetic firework stays purchasable. The recovery compass is removed from the shop entirely.
  • Behind the scenes: deploy verification now runs from a clean connection, which ends the false "deploy failed" alarms — everything those alarms hid (all of the above, plus yesterday's buy reprice) had in fact reached the server each time.

0.1m Jul 17, 2026

Milestone: the economy update is complete. R47 reworked what things sell for; this release finishes the other half — every buy price re-anchored to measured income. 398 listings rise, nothing was lowered, and no sell price changed.

  • Buy prices were calibrated when top income was believed to be ~3,000/hr; measured income is ~7,400/hr, so the whole catalogue had become 2.5–5× cheaper in real playing time than intended.
  • Four mined materials rise to their measured gathering value: coal 4.80 → 15.03, redstone 3.20 → 6.16, lapis 6.60 → 11.45, obsidian 1.03 → 8.02. Crafted items follow their ingredients (torches, lamps, redstone devices), and storage blocks cost exactly 9× their material, so the block form can never undercut a raise.
  • Food is priced on the same scale as everything else: the nutrition coefficient rises from 0.25 to 0.60 (bread 2.75 → 6.60, cooked beef 5.20 → 12.48). The pantry is balanced, not subsidized.
  • Bulk stone can no longer be sold: granite, diorite, andesite, tuff, calcite, deepslate, soul soil and end stone, plus their 34 cut and polished forms. TNT drops 100% of its loot since 1.14, so blast mining produces these in unlimited volume — the same rule that already covers cobblestone. End stone was the real leak: ~15,000 blocks/hr of risk-free end busting at 0.16 paid ~2,400/hr. Masons remain the intended buyer for bulk stone.
  • Versioning note: letters are back. The line ran 0.1a–0.1k in June, the 0.1l-RC series was absorbed into R1 by the old promotion rule, and R1–R48 were letterless revisions. Milestone releases carry letters from here on; -R revisions attach to them.

0.1-R47 Jul 17, 2026

Economy rework: income scales with risk and attention, not automation.

  • AFK-farmable output is no longer sellable to the shop: crops, wood, mob-grinder drops, iron-farm ingots, wool, dyes, and everything craftable from them (913 listings). All of it can still be bought from the shop, sold to villagers, and traded between players.
  • The /sell command is unaffected — it opens the shop's sell screen as always. What was removed is an unused parallel sell pathway from another plugin that carried its own uncalibrated default prices, plus spawner storage payouts.
  • Mining income now follows the risk ladder: ancient debris sells at 495, netherite scrap at 500, netherite ingots at 2,000. Netherite mining out-earns diamond mining, consistent with its exposure: no Fortune scaling, lava, and full-inventory risk.
  • Echo shards sell at 500 (previously 270). An ancient city raid now pays between diamond mining and netherite mining per hour.
  • Diamond is unchanged at 33.
  • Vindicators and evokers drop books instead of emeralds. Raid farms remain effective for totems, XP and gear, but no longer generate currency unattended.
  • Behind the scenes: fixed a deploy check that misreported connection failures as missing files, and a changelog publisher bug that truncated multi-line entries. If a changelog line is unclear, report it.
